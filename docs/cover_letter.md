## Significance and Novelty

Casimir/van der Waals stiction remains a primary failure mode in sub-100 nm MEMS devices,
responsible for 30–50% of field failures in electrostatically actuated structures. Existing
mitigation strategies address symptoms (surface coatings, geometric patterning) rather than
the root cause. This work presents the first computational framework that engineers the *sign*
of the Casimir force at the material level using chiral metamaterials.

**Key contributions that distinguish this work:**

1. **Correct asymmetric chiral formula**: Prior computational work (Zhao et al. 2009) applies
   the symmetric-plate formula to the Te|WTe₂ system where WTe₂ is achiral (κ₂ = 0).
   We derive and implement the physically correct asymmetric second-order scattering formula
   (Silveirinha 2010), showing that κ_crit_asym ≈ 5.8 — far outside the physical range —
   making chirality-driven repulsion *impossible* in Te|WTe₂. This is a critical correction
   to the prior literature.

2. **Symmetric Te|Te design target**: We identify the symmetric Te|Te geometry as the correct
   fabrication target, where κ_crit = 0.806 enables full Casimir repulsion and ~40% stiction
   reduction at κ_eff = 0.5 — practically achievable via oblique-angle PVD.

3. **Three-objective NSGA-II framework**: A five-variable multi-objective optimizer
   simultaneously minimizes stiction energy, device thickness, and thermal fraction,
   producing Pareto design rules unavailable from single-objective approaches.

4. **Literature-grounded two-oscillator dielectric model**: Oscillator parameters anchored to
   Caldwell & Fan (1959) and Stuke (1965) rather than fitted heuristics, enabling
   direct experimental comparison.

5. **Fully validated open-source physics engine**: The `casimir-tools` Python package
   (to be released on PyPI) implements all Lifshitz variants (isotropic, uniaxial, chiral,
   finite-T Matsubara, Drude metals) with an 82-test suite. Au/SiO₂ benchmark confirms
   Our/PC ratio ≈ 0.35–0.55 at d = 100–500 nm, consistent with published Drude-Lifshitz
   predictions (Lambrecht & Reynaud 2000).

## Scope and Audience

This manuscript is appropriate for *IEEE Transactions on Nanotechnology* as it addresses
a quantitative engineering problem (MEMS stiction elimination) using nanoscale physics
(Casimir effect, chiral metamaterials) combined with AI/optimization methods (NSGA-II) —
a combination central to the journal's scope. The results are directly actionable for MEMS
reliability engineers and metamaterial device designers.
